Paris – a rainy day

Once Louvre and Versailles are out of the way, Paris often becomes more interesting in bad weather. The city works surprisingly well through museums, passages, cafés and neighbourhoods rather than through a checklist of highlights.

Pompidou, not Les Halles

Centre Pompidou is a stronger rainy-day choice: architecture, modern art and a clear sense of place.

Musée d’Orsay

Often a more rewarding bad-weather museum than the Louvre if you want culture without being overwhelmed.

Arts et Métiers

Especially strong if technology and engineering are part of what makes Paris interesting to you.
