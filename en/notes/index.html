Collection

Notes

Not everything needs to become a full article or a carefully structured page. Some things are too small for that, too specific, or simply too useful to leave unwritten until later.

This page is meant for exactly those things: notes, small references, practical observations, fragments and reminders that tend to be useful again at some point. Some are mainly here for my own future self, others may be helpful to visitors as well.

Usefulness matters more than polish here. The goal is not completeness, but having things in one place so they can be found quickly when needed.

Areas

The notes are not meant to be a rigid archive. They are closer to a working bench: some entries are already fairly concrete, others begin as brief reminders, half-shaped ideas or a few useful references.

Linux & Administration

Practical notes on systems, configuration, troubleshooting and day-to-day operations. Linux, Apache, services and small infrastructure work all tend to benefit from having recurring patterns written down once.

  • Narrowing down SSH key problems – common failure patterns, file permissions and the places that are usually worth checking first.
  • Checking Apache configuration without guessing – a small mental checklist for vhosts, paths, permissions and where the actual problem often sits.
  • Services, logs and sequence of investigation – notes on how to avoid touching everything at once when something breaks.

Programming & Tools

Development-related notes that may be too small for their own page, but are often exactly the kind of things one wants to look up later.

  • Everyday JavaScript debugging – where to start when code runs but behaves differently from what was intended.
  • Building PHP error handling pragmatically – when logging is enough, when exceptions help, and how to keep structure without overdoing it.
  • Small helpers for day-to-day coding – tools, editor habits and patterns that age better than one-off tricks.

Databases & Data Models

Notes on SQL, structure, readability and the kinds of questions that often become interesting only after a data model has been in use for a while.

  • Getting a first feel for MySQL performance – not magic, just sensible first steps: indexes, queries, order of analysis and whether MySQL is actually the problem.
  • Writing SQL you can still read later – ways of structuring queries so they remain maintainable instead of turning into a puzzle.
  • Thinking about data models with later analysis in mind – reminders of why clean structure nearly always pays off.

Electronics, Practice & Useful Fragments

Not everything here is pure software. Some notes come from hardware, microcontrollers, measurement and practical questions that do not fit neatly into a single technical drawer.

  • Arduino and ESP32 as practical learning and working environments – where they are easy to start with, where their limits appear, and why small real projects often teach more than abstract theory.
  • Tools and references worth not having to search for again – links, fragments and resources that are not spectacular, but consistently useful.
  • Open questions and half-finished thoughts – some things deliberately remain unfinished until they have had time to grow into something clearer.

How to read this page

The notes are intentionally looser than the rest of the site. The point here is less presentation than retrievability, usefulness and not having to rediscover the same things again and again.

Some entries will probably grow into fuller topic pages later. Others will stay right here, because this form is exactly what makes them useful: short enough to scan quickly, but detailed enough to be more than a single keyword.

And otherwise

This collection will change over time. Some notes will be expanded, some merged, others may disappear again. What matters most is not completeness, but whether something turns out to be useful in practice.
