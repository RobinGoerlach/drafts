About

A few words about me

I have been working with technology, systems and programming for a long time. Some of it comes from professional work, some from curiosity, and some simply from the need to understand how things actually work.

This site is a place for projects, notes, topics and things I tend to come back to. Much of it has grown over time — some of it casually, some of it with more focus.

How it started

It didn’t start with modern systems, but with a programmable calculator and the simple idea of making repetitive work easier. From there it moved through early personal computers, expansion cards, CP/M, Turbo Pascal and various mathematical and technical tools.

Later came databases, modems, Unix systems, Linux, web servers, programming languages and many different tools. Some stayed, others were just steps along the way. Together they shaped a perspective that isn’t tied to a single stack or a single moment in time.

Maybe that’s part of what still makes it interesting: technology changes, but many of the underlying questions remain.

How I approach things

I tend to prefer solutions that remain understandable. Not everything needs to be complex just because it sounds technical. On the other hand, quick shortcuts often create problems later on.

I value clarity, structure and tools that are actually useful in the long run. That doesn’t mean everything has to be over-engineered — but a bit of calm, a bit of structure and some reflection often save more time than rushing does.

It probably shows that technology has been part of everyday life for quite a while. Not as something special, but simply as something that has grown over time.

What else has found a place here

Not everything on this site revolves around IT. Over time, other topics found their place here as well: electronics, data, gardening, plants, cooking, travel and a few things besides.

That mix is not accidental. It is simply part of how interests grow over time, and how technical questions often connect with everyday life more than one might expect.

If you prefer to look at actual work and ideas, you’ll find a selection on the projects page. That is where the focus shifts from background and context to things that were built, explored or sketched out more directly.
